The project addresses a global existential threat and a profound humanitarian crisis: the unchecked spread of Multi-Drug Resistant (MDR) and Extensively Drug-Resistant (XDR) Tuberculosis. TB affects 10.6 million people and kills over a million annually, according to the World Health Organization, which also declares it to be among the top ten global health threats [4]. It remains the world's deadliest infectious disease, and the failure of current diagnostics to provide timely, actionable resistance profiles in low-resource settings is not merely a logistical challenge; it is a systemic, moral catastrophe [5]. Existing solutions, such as the GeneXpert, are centralized, cold-chain dependent, and lack the intelligence to optimise resource allocation, resulting in millions of preventable deaths.
